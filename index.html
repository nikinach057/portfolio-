PUBLICATIONS

Intelligent GD&T symbol detection in mechanical drawings: a comparative study of YOLOv11, Faster R-CNN, and RetinaNet for quality assurance

Authors: Tadigotla Narendra Reddy, Nitesh Kumar, Nachappa Pemmanda Ponnappa, Nagasiri Mohana, Prakash Vinod, Mervin A. Herbert, Shrikantha S. Rao

Journal: Journal of Intelligent Manufacturing (2025)

This research presents a comparative analysis of three state-of-the-art deep learning models for automated GD&T symbol detection in CAD engineering drawings. The study addresses the need for cost-effective, indigenous solutions for SMEs and MSMEs in quality automation tasks, demonstrating that YOLOv11 provides the optimal balance between detection accuracy and real-time performance.

Overall equipment efficiency module and losses using machine-to-machine connectivity for smart factory

Authors: Tadigotla Narendra Reddy, Nachappa Pemmanda Ponnappa, Prakash Vinod, Mervin A. Herbert, Shrikantha S. Rao

Journal: ISSS Journal of Micro and Smart Systems, Volume 14, pages 87–99 (2025)

This study focuses on implementing Overall Equipment Effectiveness (OEE) using OPC UA (Open Platform Communications Unified Architecture) in smart manufacturing settings. The research combines data from CNC machines with plant schedules to demonstrate real-time OEE calculation and identify operational losses. The solution can be deployed on-premises or in the cloud to automate OEE calculation processes, providing insights for productivity gains and operational flow improvements.

Development of Low-Cost IIoT Solution for Smart Factories in MSME Industries: Utilizing Current Measurements for Machine and Factory Monitoring

Authors: T. Narendra Reddy, Shri Prakash Vinod, P. P. Nachappa, Mervin A. Herbert, Shrikantha S. Rao

Conference: Industry 4.0 and Advanced Manufacturing (I-4AM 2024), Lecture Notes in Mechanical Engineering

This paper presents the development of an affordable IIoT solution for productivity monitoring in MSME industries. The solution leverages cost-effective hardware for sensing, a controller for data acquisition and analysis, and an edge gateway for cloud storage. The system provides insights into factory and machine statuses, facilitates productivity tracking, and enables automated reporting through Email and SMS with customized daily and monthly reports.

An Industry 4.0 Approach: Data Acquisition and Machine Monitoring for Welding Machines

Authors: T. Narendra Reddy, Nachappa Pemmanda Ponnappa, P. Prasad, Prakash Vinod, Mervin A. Herbert, Shrikantha S. Rao

Conference: Smart Materials and Manufacturing Technologies for Sustainable Development (SME 2023), Advances in Science, Technology & Innovation

This research introduces an innovative application of Industry 4.0 principles in welding by employing IIoT-based performance monitoring equipment. The developed solution can be used for any kind of welding machines including legacy welding machines, delivering real-time updates on shop floor welding processes using Operational Technology (OT) and Information Technology (IT) with hall effect sensors and voltage transducers connected to PLCs.
